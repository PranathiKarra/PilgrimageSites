About Tawang

Tawang is a town in the Tawang district of the northeastern state of Arunachal Pradesh, India. It is located at an elevation of approximately 10,000 feet (3,048 meters) above sea level and is known for its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and religious significance.The town is surrounded by the Himalayan mountain range, offering breathtaking views of snow-capped peaks and lush green valleys.

Tawang Monastery is located at an altitude of approximately 10,000 feet in the Tawang town of Arunachal Pradesh, near the India-China border. The monastery is nestled amidst the scenic Himalayan mountains, providing breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes.Situated atop a hill at an altitude of approximately 10,000 feet, Tawang Monastery overlooks the Tawang-Chu valley.The location provides stunning panoramic views of the surrounding Himalayan mountains and the Tawang town.

About Temple

Tawang Monastery was founded by Merak Lama Lodre Gyatso in the 17th century, in the year 1680-81. It is one of the largest monasteries in India.The monastery has historical significance and has played a crucial role in the spread of Buddhism in the region.Tawang Monastery is a significant center for the Gelug school of Mahayana Buddhism, the same school to which the Dalai Lama belongs.The monastery is dedicated to Avalokitesvara, the Buddhist deity of compassion. It also houses various precious manuscripts, scriptures, and artifacts related to Buddhism.

About Tawang Monastery

    The name "Tawang" is derived from two Tibetan words: "Ta" meaning horse and "Wang" meaning chosen. Therefore, Tawang translates to "Chosen Horse" in Tibetan. The name is believed to have been given in reference to the horse that Merak Lama Lodre Gyatso, the founder of Tawang Monastery, rode when he arrived at the location to establish the monastery in the 17th century.The choice of the name "Tawang" might also carry symbolic or religious significance within the context of Tibetan Buddhism, as names often have specific meanings or associations in Tibetan culture. The name has become iconic, representing not just the monastery but also the town and the broader Tawang district in Arunachal Pradesh, India.
